In Whose Interest? Shadows Over the Hungarian Residency Bond Program

Client

Boldizsár Nagy / Transparency International Hungary

Role

Art Director, Graphic Designer, Cover Illustrator & Interior Layout Designer

Year

2016

The Challenge

Design a powerful and thought-provoking cover (along with interior layout) for a serious investigative publication examining the Hungarian Residency Bond Program. The book needed to convey themes of power, influence, opacity, and political interest while maintaining a professional, credible tone suitable for an academic and policy-making audience.

The Process

I was responsible for the complete artistic direction of the project. After researching the content, I developed a strong conceptual approach using chess pieces as a central metaphor for strategy, power, and manipulation. I created a dramatic high-contrast composition featuring a large red pawn in the foreground against a dark background with a black queen silhouette, symbolizing hidden influence and unequal power dynamics. I then designed the full cover system and established a clean, readable interior layout to support the dense investigative text.

The Solution

A striking, minimalist cover dominated by a bold red chess pawn and a shadowy black queen, creating immediate visual tension and intellectual intrigue. The typography is strong and authoritative, while the overall dark, high-contrast aesthetic reinforces the serious investigative nature of the book. The interior follows a disciplined grid with clear hierarchy for optimal readability.

Results / Impact

The design effectively captured the critical tone of the publication and helped establish a strong, memorable visual identity for this Transparency International-supported report. It has been well-received in academic and policy circles for its powerful symbolic approach.
